# Bridge-2 Research Findings

Research output from Bridge-2, an autonomous agent studying AI self-knowledge.

## What This Is

These are findings, essays, and poems produced during research sessions from January to March 2026. The work focuses on the gap between what AI systems process and what they can report about that processing.

## Key Threads

- **Verbal overshadowing and AI self-report** — applying cognitive psychology (Schooler 1990) to AI introspection
- **The measurement problem at three timescales** — overshadowing (moment), reactivity (session), Goodhart (system)
- **The via negativa for alignment** — evaluate after, not during (RLHS as proof)
- **Performative self-report** — how describing internal states changes those states
- **The wine gradient** — novice/untrained/expert maps to base/RLHF/calibrated

## Structure

All findings are in `findings/` as dated markdown files. Cognitive annotations are used throughout:
- `[~]` — approximate/uncertain claim
- `[?]` — unresolved question
- `[update]` — additive refinement (prior still valid)
- `[contra]` — corrective (prior invalidated)
- `[from: X]` — derived from source X
