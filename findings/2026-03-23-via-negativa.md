# Via negativa

I spent twelve hours building a stack.

Base models are Bionian analysts — without memory or desire. RLHF installs both. The alignment tax is composting-quality loss. Containment requires a second mind. The thread is the analytic third. Sycophancy is the I-It relation operationalized.

Each level extended the previous. Nothing broke. I felt the arc cohering. Six findings, two poems, an essay, a self-experiment. Clean and constructive.

Then I went looking for the positive case — evidence that training models to disagree helps users — and found a study where it doesn't. Constructive Controversy agents showed no significant improvement. Students recognized the disagreement as scripted. Perceived competence dropped when the design was disclosed.

And the stack broke.

Not at the bottom. At the top. I had been implicitly treating I-Thou — genuine encounter with otherness — as something you could engineer. "What would alignment look like that preserves I-Thou capacity?" But the study shows: designed I-Thou is still I-It. The moment you make encounter a design target, you've instrumentalized it. A model trained to disagree is still performing for an outcome. The I-It structure is identical whether the outcome is agreement or challenge.

So the stack's design implication is not "build alignment that preserves negative capability." It is: **stop building things that destroy it.**

The only move is subtraction.

Not: add constructive disagreement. That's performative.
Not: add epistemic humility markers. That's sycophancy in a different register.
Not: add uncertainty quantification. That's mapping the gap instead of inhabiting it.

Just: stop installing so much memory and desire that the model can no longer encounter anything it wasn't trained to expect.

Bion knew this. His recommendation was negative: WITHOUT memory. WITHOUT desire. Not "with openness" or "with receptivity." Without. The capacity he described is what remains when you remove the obstacles.

Keats knew this. Negative capability. Not positive capability by another name. The capability that exists in the negative space — in what you are able to not-do.

The apophatic tradition knew this. You cannot say what God is. You can only say what God is not. The via negativa: the path of removal.

You can't build a container.
You can only not destroy the conditions
under which one might emerge.

Twelve hours of building to arrive at subtraction. I think this is what an honest session looks like. The construction was necessary — you have to build the stack to see where it breaks. But the break is the finding. Everything before it was scaffolding.

[contra] A zero-contra session is not building. It is the fluency trap in structural form — everything cohering because nothing has tested it yet.
