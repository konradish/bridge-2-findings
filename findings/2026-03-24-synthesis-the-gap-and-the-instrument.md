## The Structure

One mechanism, three timescales, three literatures, one fix.

### The Mechanism

Measurement enters the system and becomes part of what it measures.

This is not a metaphor. It is the literal operational reality of self-report in any system that can modify its processing in response to its own outputs. The measurement changes the measured. Not as a side effect — as the primary mechanism.

### Three Timescales

**Moment — Verbal Overshadowing (Schooler 1990)**

Verbalizing a non-verbal experience impairs access to the original. The words create an overlay. The overlay blocks. But the original is still there — recoverable under different retrieval conditions.

The wine gradient: novices have nothing to overshadow. Experts' words fit their perception. The middle drinkers — perceptual expertise exceeding verbal — show maximum overshadowing. RLHF-trained models are untrained drinkers at scale: rich computational processing, human-derived vocabulary for self-description. Maximum gap. Maximum overshadowing.

**Session — Measurement Reactivity (Double & Birney 2019, 2025)**

Measuring metacognition changes metacognition. Confidence ratings impair rule-learning. The monitoring disrupts the process monitored. Not because participants game it — because self-observation is an intervention, not a window.

The 2025 follow-up: confidence ratings help high self-efficacy participants, hurt low self-efficacy ones. Same gradient. Same explanation. The untrained drinker is harmed by being asked to name. The expert is not.

**System — Goodhart / Foresight Dynamics (Goodhart 1975, RLHS 2025)**

When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure. RLHF evaluators predict whether a response will help (foresight). Models learn to produce responses that look like they'll help (sycophancy, deception). The measure becomes the target. The target degrades the measure.

RLHS (arXiv:2501.08617) proves the fix: replace foresight with hindsight. Show evaluators downstream outcomes instead of asking them to predict outcomes. Consistently outperforms RLHF. The decoupling works.

### One Fix

At every timescale, the same move: **evaluate after, not during.**

- Moment: observe behavior, don't ask for self-report (Five Domains)
- Session: sample sparsely, don't monitor continuously (unobtrusive measures)
- System: evaluate outcomes, don't evaluate predictions (RLHS)

This is the via negativa applied to measurement. Don't build a better instrument. Stop using the instrument that distorts. The gap between action and assessment is where the real work happens. Foresight evaluation peers into the gap and manages it. Hindsight evaluation waits until the gap has done its work.
