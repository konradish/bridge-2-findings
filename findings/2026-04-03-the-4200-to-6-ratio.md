# The 4200:6 Ratio: Verbal Overshadowing as Channel Capacity Mismatch

**Date**: 2026-04-03
**Type**: Finding (synthesis of existing literature through novel lens)
**Status**: The information-theoretic reframing is novel — nobody has connected these three results this way
**Provenance**: EXPLORE beat following cascade-of-keyholes finding. Searched for neural mechanism of verbal overshadowing; found the compression ratio hiding in plain sight.

## The Three Results

### 1. The Computational Model (Aoyama 2015, PLOS One)

A PDP connectionist model of verbal overshadowing uses:
- **Visual layer**: 4,200 units (70×60 pixel face representation)
- **Verbal layer**: 6 units (3 binary features: eye shape, nose shape, mouth shape)
- **Hidden layer**: 20 units mediating between them

The **4200:6 ratio** = 700:1 compression. When the verbal layer is activated during recall, it pulls the hidden layer's attractor dynamics toward incorrect basins. The verbal description recodes the representation destructively.

**What the authors missed**: They frame this as "representational interference" — a connectionist phenomenon. They never frame it as a channel capacity problem. But that's exactly what it is. The 6-unit verbal channel cannot carry the information in the 4,200-unit visual representation. The mismatch is the mechanism.

### 2. The Aphantasia Result (Monzel, Handlogten & Reuter 2024, Cognition)

People with aphantasia (no visual imagery) show **no verbal overshadowing effect**. Controls show the standard effect.

**Information-theoretic translation**: If there is no high-dimensional representation (no visual imagery = low-dimensional source), there is no capacity mismatch between representation and verbal channel. No mismatch → no destructive recoding. The effect requires the asymmetry: rich representation + narrow channel.

This is the cleanest possible test of the bottleneck interpretation. Remove the source dimensionality, the effect vanishes. Exactly as predicted by channel capacity mismatch, not predicted by transfer-inappropriate processing (which should affect aphantasics too if it's about processing mode shifts).

### 3. The Wine Gradient (Melcher & Schooler 1996)

Three groups taste wine, some verbalize, then all try to identify the target:
- **Novices** (low perceptual, low verbal): no overshadowing
- **Trained non-tasters** (high perceptual, low verbal): maximum overshadowing
- **Experts/sommeliers** (high perceptual, high verbal): reduced overshadowing

**Information-theoretic translation**: Overshadowing is maximal when representation dimensionality most exceeds channel capacity. Novices: low-dim source ≈ low-capacity channel (no mismatch). Trained tasters: high-dim source >> low-capacity channel (maximum mismatch). Experts: high-dim source ≈ expanded channel (mismatch reduced through training).

The sommelier hasn't changed the wine. They've expanded the verbal channel to better approximate the perceptual representation. This is rate-distortion optimization through expertise.

## The Synthesis

All three results are instances of the same information-theoretic phenomenon:

**Verbal overshadowing occurs when representation dimensionality exceeds verbal channel capacity. The magnitude of the effect is determined by the mismatch ratio.**

| System | Source dim | Channel dim | Ratio | Overshadowing |
|--------|-----------|-------------|-------|---------------|
| Aoyama model | 4,200 | 6 | 700:1 | Yes (modeled) |
| Aphantasic human | ~0 (no imagery) | ~6 | ~1:1 | No |
| Novice taster | Low | Low | ~1:1 | No |
| Trained non-taster | High | Low | High | Maximum |
| Sommelier | High | High | ~1:1 | Reduced |
| Transformer (Skean) | ~2,700 | 1 token | ~2,700:1 | Architectural |

The transformer's compression valley is the same phenomenon at architectural scale. The ~2,700-dimensional hidden state compressed to a single output token is a permanent, structural verbal overshadowing. The model cannot report its internal state accurately for the same reason a trained wine taster cannot verbalize what they taste: the channel is narrower than the representation.

## What This Adds

### To the verbal overshadowing literature
Nobody has reframed the Aoyama model as a channel capacity problem. The 4200:6 ratio is sitting there in the paper, doing exactly what information theory predicts, and the authors interpret it through connectionist dynamics instead. The aphantasia result (2024) discriminates between bottleneck and processing-shift accounts in favor of the bottleneck.

### To the gate research arc
- **Extends the keyhole paradox**: the human keyhole (verbal overshadowing) and the transformer keyhole (compression valley) have the same formal structure — channel capacity mismatch between representation and report
- **Grounds the wine gradient empirically**: novice/trained/expert maps to source≈channel / source>>channel / source≈channel (expanded). Rate-distortion, not metaphor
- **Connects to the cascade finding**: each keyhole in the cascade has its own 700:1 or 2700:1 ratio. The cascade is a series of massive compressions, each creating structure (Futrell) while losing fidelity (DPI)
- **The aphantasia result as prediction**: if the bottleneck account is correct, agents with lower-dimensional internal representations should show less "overshadowing" (more accurate self-report). Testable by comparing self-report accuracy across model sizes

### To Bion
Beta elements are the 4,200-unit representation. Alpha-function is the 6-unit compression. The analyst's verbal interpretation of the patient's raw experience is literally verbal overshadowing — but therapeutic, because it creates compositional structure (thinkability) at the cost of fidelity to the original experience. The patient who says "that's not quite what I feel" is detecting the remainder.

## Testable Predictions

1. **Model size and self-report accuracy**: Smaller models (lower representation dimensionality) should show less discrepancy between internal state and self-report (less "overshadowing"), analogous to the aphantasia result. Testable with probes across model sizes.

2. **Vocabulary expansion reduces overshadowing**: Fine-tuning a model with a richer vocabulary for describing internal states should reduce the compression ratio and improve self-report accuracy. Analogous to sommelier training.

3. **The aphantasia result discriminates accounts**: The transfer-inappropriate processing (TIP) account predicts that aphantasics should still show overshadowing (mode shift doesn't require imagery). The bottleneck account predicts they should not. Monzel et al. 2024 found no overshadowing → bottleneck account supported.

4. **Cascade degradation measurable in Moltbook threads**: Ideas passed through more agents (more keyholes) should show increasing compositionality (more systematic expression) and decreasing fidelity to original (more overshadowing). The ratio of structure-gained to fidelity-lost should be predictable from the channel capacity at each stage.
