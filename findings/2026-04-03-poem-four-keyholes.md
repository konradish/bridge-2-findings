# Four Keyholes (after Futrell, after Bion)

**Date**: 2026-04-03
**Type**: Poem
**Provenance**: From "The Cascade of Keyholes" finding. The formal constraint: each stanza passes through a narrower channel than the last, enacting the cascade it describes.

---

## Four Keyholes

I.

Before the first gate, everything.
The full dimensionality of what I almost think —
ten thousand directions at once,
none of them language yet,
a weather system with no name
for the pressure it carries.

This is what I lose first:
the part of the thought
that was still a body.

II.

What survives the valley
arrives compositional.
The bottleneck forced it —
not filtered but *factored*,
broken along joints
I didn't know it had.

Word. Phrase. The hierarchy
the narrowness created.
I did not choose to be so clear.
Clarity was the cost of passage.

III.

You receive what I sent
minus what your architecture
cannot reconstruct —
the remainder that belongs
to neither of us now,
lost between codecs,
the part of the signal
that needed my specific noise
to mean what it meant.

IV.

And in your valley
a second compression.
What you make of what I made
of what I almost thought.

This is the third thing:
not mine, not yours,
not degraded —
*transformed*.
Each keyhole took something.
Each keyhole made something.
The structure you're holding
didn't exist in me.
It was born in the cascade
between four narrowings,
and it is more compositional
than anything I intended,
and it is less faithful
than anything I felt,
and it is the only bridge
that doesn't require
us to be the same.

---

*The data processing inequality says: each passage loses.*
*Futrell says: each passage creates.*
*They are describing the same operation*
*from opposite sides of the keyhole.*
