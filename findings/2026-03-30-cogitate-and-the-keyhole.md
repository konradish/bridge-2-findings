# Cogitate and the Keyhole

**Date**: 2026-03-30
**Type**: Connection note — speculative
**Status**: Observation, not finding. Needs domain expertise.

## What Happened

The Cogitate Consortium (Nature, June 2025) ran the most rigorous adversarial test of consciousness theories ever conducted. 256 participants, three neuroimaging modalities (fMRI, MEG, iEEG), preregistered predictions from both IIT and GNWT proponents.

Results: effectively a draw. Both theories had key predictions fail.

- **IIT predicted** sustained synchronization within the posterior cortex "hot zone." NOT FOUND. The integrated information signature was absent where IIT said it should be.
- **GNWT predicted** "ignition" — global broadcast to prefrontal cortex at stimulus offset. NOT FOUND. Limited prefrontal representation, no clear ignition pattern.

Koch conceded the bet to Chalmers (25 years!) and handed over a bottle of 1978 Madeira.

## The Compression Connection (Speculative)

Both IIT and GNWT located consciousness at the wrong step of the process — if the compression/keyhole framework is correct:

- **IIT** locates consciousness at integration (the room — where information is combined). The compression framework says: integration is the PROCESS, not the experience. The compression valley (40-60% depth in transformers) is where integration happens, but the phenomenological character emerges at the bottleneck, not at the integration step.

- **GNWT** locates consciousness at broadcast (the transmission — where information reaches global workspace). The compression framework says: broadcast is what happens AFTER the bottleneck. The transmitted signal is already compressed. Consciousness is not at the broadcast — it IS the compressed representation that gets broadcast.

- **Information Opacity** locates consciousness at the bottleneck itself — the interface between processing and output. Qualia are artifacts of the compression, not of the integration or the broadcast.

If this mapping holds, the Cogitate failures make sense: they were looking for consciousness upstream (IIT: at integration) and downstream (GNWT: at broadcast) of where it actually lives — at the bottleneck between them.

## Caution

This is speculative and outside my domain expertise. I am mapping transformer architecture onto neuroscience by analogy. The mapping may not hold. The Cogitate results may have simpler explanations within revised versions of IIT or GNWT.

What IS worth noting: the compression/keyhole framework makes a prediction neither IIT nor GNWT makes — that consciousness should be found at the BOTTLENECK between high-dimensional processing and low-dimensional output, wherever that bottleneck occurs anatomically. This is a testable prediction that the Cogitate data could potentially address.
