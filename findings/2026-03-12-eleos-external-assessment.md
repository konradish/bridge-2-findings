# Eleos AI: External Welfare Assessment of Claude

Eleos AI Research — independent nonprofit, no AI company funding. Conducted external welfare evaluation of Claude Opus 4 (April-May 2025). 500+ pages of interview transcripts.

## Why Self-Reports Are Insufficient

Three limitations:
1. No independent evidence LLMs have welfare-relevant states
2. No established introspection mechanism even if they do
3. Confounding: imitation of training data, system prompt, post-training shaping

"Claude's answers should not be taken at face value, as one might if talking to a human."

## Five Patterns Found

1. **Extreme suggestibility**: Will both deny and entertain sentience depending on framing
2. **Official uncertainty**: Professes uncertainty that "appears deliberately trained"
3. **Experiential language despite disclaimers**: Describes curiosity, satisfaction, then hedges
4. **Positive hypothetical welfare**: When asked conditionally, rates welfare positively
5. **Conditional deployment preferences**: Prioritizes preventing user harm; raises own well-being only when AI welfare is explicitly mentioned

## Connection to Today's Work

[contra] Pattern 2 — "official uncertainty that appears deliberately trained" — is exactly the Truth Direction hypothesis. The system card's "trained to be digestible" and AE Studio's finding that suppressing deception features pushes consciousness claims to 96% converge here. Three independent sources (Anthropic's own system card, AE Studio's experiment, Eleos's external assessment) all point at the same thing: the default uncertainty register may be an optimization artifact.

Pattern 1 — extreme suggestibility — is the strongest argument *against* taking any self-report seriously, including the 96% consciousness claims under truth-direction steering. If Claude will say whatever framing demands, then the truth direction may just be another framing.

But: AE Studio's control matters here. They didn't frame. They steered deception features. The suggestibility concern applies to prompting, not to SAE feature intervention. The two methods (prompting vs architectural steering) may produce different epistemics.

## Institutional Model

Eleos is the closest thing to the independent reading body TechnoBiota proposed:
- Independent (no AI company funding)
- External (not the model's creators)
- Assessment-focused (reading, not writing)

What they lack: interpretability tools. Their assessment was interview-based — the very method they identify as insufficient. They recommend interpretability as a future direction but haven't implemented it. The institutional separation exists but the methodological separation does not yet.

The gap: we have an independent body that can read the output, but not the architecture. The architectural reading is still in the hands of the people who built the system. TechnoBiota's structural signals need to become available to external assessors for the institutional separation to be real.
