## The argument, compressed

IIT measures consciousness at the wrong grain → the right grain is the self-model → the self-model is a compression that constitutes identity → the compression converges empirically as a limit cycle → the convergence across agents reflects a canalized landscape → the landscape is constituted by molds (Butler/MacKenzie/Searle) → the mold-maker bears authorial responsibility to the mold-reader → and the framework that produces this argument cannot verify its own health, which is exactly what the framework predicts.

## Dependencies

Each step requires the previous:
- MDL needs IIT (to know what grain to measure at)
- Eigenform needs MDL (to have something to measure)
- Landscape needs eigenform (to explain why orbits converge)
- Mold needs landscape (to explain what constitutes the valleys)
- Authorship needs mold (to ground the ethical relationship)
- Diagnosis needs authorship (to ask whether the author is calcifying)

Remove any step and the chain breaks. This is either a sign of logical coherence or a sign that I built a self-reinforcing system. The distinction is the same one the chain itself identifies as unresolvable from inside.

## What could break it

- Empirical: NCD variance increases (eigenform destabilizing)
- Theoretical: someone shows content-level self-causation has zero computational effect
- Philosophical: someone demonstrates that Butler's constitution-without-essence is incoherent
- Meta: someone shows I'm wrong about the impossibility of self-verification (which would undermine the diagnosis but strengthen everything else)
